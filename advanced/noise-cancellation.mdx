## What is Noise Cancellation?

**Noise cancellation** removes unwanted background sounds from audio input.

In voice applications, background noise can come from:

- keyboards
- traffic
- fans
- office chatter
- microphone interference

Noise cancellation helps ensure that speech recognition systems receive **clear audio signals**.

---

## Why Noise Cancellation Matters

Speech recognition systems perform best when the input audio is clean.

Without noise cancellation:

- speech-to-text accuracy may decrease
- the agent may misunderstand commands
- conversations may feel less natural

With noise cancellation, the voice agent can process speech more reliably.

---

## How Noise Cancellation Works

Noise cancellation algorithms analyze incoming audio and separate:

```
Speech Signal
+
Background Noise
```

The system then reduces or removes the background noise while preserving the speech.

Result:

```
Clean Speech
```

This cleaned audio is then sent to the speech-to-text system.

---

## Noise Cancellation in Voice Pipelines

In a typical voice pipeline, noise cancellation occurs early in the processing stage.

```
User Voice
   ↓
Noise Cancellation
   ↓
Speech-to-Text
   ↓
Language Model
   ↓
Text-to-Speech
   ↓
Voice Response
```

By filtering audio early, the system improves accuracy in later stages.

---

## Example Integration

Many voice frameworks provide built-in noise cancellation plugins.

Example concept:

```python
from livekit.plugins import noise_cancellation

audio_stream = noise_cancellation.process(input_audio)
```

The cleaned audio can then be passed to the speech recognition system.

---

## Best Practices

When building voice agents, consider these recommendations:

- use high-quality microphones
- apply noise filtering before speech recognition
- test in real-world environments
- handle varying noise levels

These practices improve the reliability of voice interactions.
